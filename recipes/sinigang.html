SINIGANG

Finished Product

Description

Sinigang is a popular and savory Filipino soup known for its distinct sour flavor. It is a comforting and hearty dish that features a tamarind-based broth, although other souring agents such as guava, green mango, or calamansi may also be used. Sinigang is often prepared with a variety of meats, such as pork, beef, shrimp, or fish, and it typically includes an assortment of vegetables.

Ingredients

  • Meat of choice (pork ribs, pork belly, beef shanks, shrimp, or fish)
  • Tamarind-based sinigang mix, tamarind pulp, or other souring agents
  • Water
  • Vegetables (radish, eggplant, string beans, water spinach, and/or green leafy vegetables like kangkong or bok choy)
  • Onions, peeled and sliced
  • Tomatoes, quartered
  • Green chili peppers (siling pansigang), optional for added heat
  • Fish sauce (patis) or salt, to taste
  • Fish sauce (patis) or salt, to taste

Steps

  1. Prepare the broth

    In a pot, combine water and the chosen meat. Bring it to a boil, then lower the heat to simmer until the meat is partially cooked.

  2. Add vegetables and aromatics

    Add the sliced onions and quartered tomatoes to the pot. Include the tamarind-based sinigang mix or tamarind pulp to achieve the desired level of sourness. Other souring agents like guava, green mango, or calamansi can be used as alternatives.

  3. Season the soup

    Season the soup with fish sauce or salt according to taste. Add black pepper for additional flavor. Some variations might include a hint of sweetness from corn or taro.

  4. Add vegetables

    Once the meat is nearly tender, add the radish, eggplant, string beans, and other vegetables. Allow them to cook until they are tender but not overcooked.

  5. Adjust seasoning

    Taste the soup and adjust the seasoning as needed. You can add more fish sauce or salt for saltiness and more tamarind for sourness.

  6. Serve

    Serve the sinigang hot in bowls over steamed rice. It's common to enjoy sinigang with a side of fish sauce and calamansi for additional flavor.
