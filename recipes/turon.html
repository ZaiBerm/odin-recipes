TURON

Finished Product

Description

Turon, also known as banana lumpia or banana spring rolls, is a popular Filipino snack or dessert. It is made by wrapping slices of ripe banana and sometimes with a slice of jackfruit in a spring roll wrapper, then frying the roll until it becomes golden and crispy. Turon is often sweetened with caramelized sugar, giving it a delightful combination of crunchy texture and sweet flavor.

Ingredients

  • Ripe bananas (usually saba or cooking bananas)
  • Jackfruit slices (optional)
  • Spring roll wrappers (also known as lumpia wrappers)
  • Brown sugar
  • Cooking oil for frying

Steps

  1. Prepare the ingredients

    Peel and slice the ripe bananas lengthwise. If using jackfruit, cut it into thin strips.

  2. Assemble the turon

    Lay out a spring roll wrapper and place a slice of banana (and a slice of jackfruit, if desired) near one edge. Sprinkle brown sugar over the banana.

  3. Roll the turon

    Fold the sides of the wrapper over the banana and roll it tightly from the bottom to the top, sealing the edges with a little water to ensure the turon stays closed.

  4. Caramelize the turon

    In a separate pan, melt brown sugar over medium heat until it caramelizes. Some variations may include adding a bit of water to make a caramel syrup.

  5. Fry the turon

    Heat cooking oil in a deep pan for frying. Carefully place the assembled turon into the hot oil, frying until it becomes golden brown and crispy.

  6. Drain and cool

    Remove the fried turon from the oil and let them drain on paper towels to absorb excess oil. Allow them to cool for a few minutes.

  7. Serve

    Serve turon while still warm. The crispy exterior contrasts with the sweet and soft interior, creating a delightful combination of textures. Turon is often enjoyed as a snack or dessert in the Philippines.
