ADOBO

Finished Product

Description

Adobo is a popular Filipino dish known for its savory and slightly tangy flavor profile. It is a cooking method that involves marinating meat, usually chicken or pork, in a mixture of vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, and various spices, and then simmering it until tender. Adobo is considered the unofficial national dish of the Philippines and holds a special place in Filipino cuisine.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs chicken pieces (drumsticks, thighs, or a combination)
  • 1 onion, peeled and sliced thinly
  • 1 head of garlic, peeled and minced/li>
  • 1 cup soy sauce
  • 1 cup white vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon black peppercorns, crushed
  • 3 bay leaves
  • 1 tablespoon cooking oil
  • 1 cup water
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: 1 teaspoon sugar for a slightly sweet balance

Steps

  1. Marinate the chicken

    In a large bowl, combine the chicken, soy sauce, minced garlic, crushed peppercorns, and bay leaves. Allow the chicken to marinate for at least 30 minutes to an hour, or even overnight for more flavor.

  2. Sauté the Aromatics

    Heat oil in a large pan or pot over medium heat. Add the sliced onions and sauté until translucent.

  3. Cook the chicken

    Add the marinated chicken to the pot, including the marinade. Allow the chicken to brown slightly on all sides.

  4. Simmer

    Pour in the vinegar and add water. Allow the mixture to come to a boil, then reduce the heat to low, cover, and let it simmer until the chicken is tender. This usually takes about 30 to 45 minutes.

  5. Adjust seasoning

    Taste the adobo and adjust the seasoning. You can add salt, pepper, or a teaspoon of sugar for a slightly sweet balance. The adobo should have a good balance of savory, tangy, and slightly sweet flavors.

  6. Serve

    Serve the Chicken Adobo over steamed rice. The sauce is flavorful and can be drizzled over the rice. Enjoy!
